My non-chronological journey of becoming a designer

Contents

Lately, I work for SPIDER. They improve the lives of the poor and marginalised through tech. Here I learned content-driven design while doing it, by turning academic reports into short, scannable text, and designing a website around that. After finding a new way of describing SPIDERs work, I created a content strategy and a new website.

Before that, I had tried my hand at freelancing, which I still do. Check out my services:

My first freelance job was for community builder Haaartland. With clear communication to my client, I analysed their flow to make it shorter. “But is shorter better?” I thought: My interviews showed that the users wanted to create a usable community page, before inviting people. So I designed a better order to do things.

Having done mostly analysis and research, I realised my visual design skills needed polishing, so I delved into graphic design to get the principles right.

Inspired, I designed this concept:

Quince, a music streaming service for the curious

I could finally apply my aesthetic sensibility from drawing and painting to digital work.

Speaking of background, I have always loved text. That’s why I studied Literature at university, and why I focus on text and communication in my designs. Expanding on the Quince concept, I wrote a Tone of Voice guide:
