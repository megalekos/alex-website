Project:
SPIDER

Background and Challenge

This development organisation needed a better way of describing themselves and their work, showcasing strengths and processes. The project ran for 6 months.

Result

Digital strategies, writing guides and new layouts for the website all contribute to telling the story of SPIDER. A design system that guides the novice designer paired with a workshop enables continous development of the site.

Process

To get a picture of how SPIDER’s work is done, I read project reports and conducted interviews with program managers. The insights were then distilled into visual models and text summaries.

The website had to be editable by the team, thus made in Wordpress. Since they didn’t have experience in formal UX processes, prototyping and so on, I realized a Design system in Figma would be helpful in the future for them to create consistant designs.

I then held a workshop in using Figma, focusing on wireframing and prototyping.
With summaries and service definitions at hand, I could also design a digital strategy, comprising of communication goals, channels and appropriate formats.

Conclusion

SPIDER now has some foundations on how to incorporate design into their processes, having all their perspectives melded into an actionable digital strategy. With the writing guides they can more uniformly tell the story about their work, and they can present this coherently on a website with the help of a design system.
